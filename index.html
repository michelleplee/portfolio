MERIT

Presented at SOFA Connect 2016
Received Funding from SOFA Chicago for Fabrication

Domocracy is a seating installation inspired by student protests at UC Davis over recent years. My team settled on highlighting the question:

How might we create a space that encourages freedom of speech?

We looked to structures on our own campus—the UC Davis Domes—to inspire the design of the installation’s form. Every element of the design references the core value of free speech within our community while taking inspiration from some key components that make the UC Davis campus unique. This project was created as a submission to SOFA Connect 2016 . The prompt asked us to create seating that represented our school. It had to meet the requirements of: staging, lighting, and seating.

The wooden structural framework of Domocracy has been inspired by flat-pack construction methods and Japanese joinery, techniques that involves interlocking materials together to maximize stability and material-efficiency. After researching and studying existing techniques, we began to explore ways in which our design could maintain itself while supporting human weight. Our earliest cardboard models developed into rapid prototypes on the CNC mill to find which design is most reliable. Many of our early models included individually designed seats attempting to stimulate various points of repose and posture. The original idea was for the seating itself to stimulate discourse within the space, but we felt that was ineffective and began to look too cluttered. We felt this design did not create a cohesive, unified seating area and instead redesigned the seating into a singular, adaptable profile.

Domocracy incorporates responsive lighting. The volume of the conversation and the presence of people engaging in conversation within the area activate LED strips located beneath the seats. The programming language, Arduino, was utilized for this.

Presented at "Mind Grown" Pop Up Exhibition @ the John Natsoulas Gallery, December 7th 2016

FRTZ is a conceptual project that places viewers within the story of a merger of two companies — Fritz Co. and Fritz Inc. This interactive installation takes the form of a hiring kiosk. It tests the limts of the visitor's ethics against a sinister company history which mirrors the story of today’s leading food and medicine corporations.

HackDavis 2016 Team

HackDavis 2016 was the first major collegiate hackathon at UC Davis completely run by students. In Spring 2016, over 450 students, hackers, and creatives came together for 36 hours of hacking. We provided a platform to bring together the most talented students in California to address the world’s most pressing social issues; asking ourselves:

How might we facilitate creation for social good?

There were three main tracks to our hackathon: Environment, Health & Wellness, and Education. With these 3 tracks, we hoped to inspire change and cultivate a growing hacker community dedicated to social change at UC Davis.

As the director of Design for this project, I created a professional branding system that was used to communicate with sponsors, generating over $50,000 in support. We received funding from top companies such as Google, Amazon, IBM, and more. After establishing our professional appearance, I hired a small team of designers to aid in creating the marketing materials for the event.

At the end of the event, we gave out over $10,000 in prizes, and donated a matching amount to three charities of our choosing.
